Just Noticable Difference

About this project

"In this study, we focus on Just Noticeable Difference (JND), a crucial concept shedding light on how people of different times, accept/reject different societal concepts/norms.Just Noticeable Difference (JND) is the smallest discernible difference between two stimuli that an individual can perceive. It represents the threshold at which a change becomes noticeable to a person, varying based on stimuli and individual sensitivity.In order to access our survey link click the button below".

Opinion on Smoking

55% of respondents consider smoking as absurd, while 33% regard it as not normal.All older respondents expressed that smoking is either absurd or not normal.

Attitude Towards Cross-Dressing

The majority of respondents considered cross-dressing as either absurd or not normal.Only 2 entries indicated a neutral stance on this issue and they were of the younger respondents.

Attitude Towards Abusive Language in Teen Conversations

7.8% of respondents considered abusive language as normal.A significant portion (30% and 37%) regarded it as neutral or not normal respectively.The rest considered it as absurd.

Significance of Gender Roles in Relationships

Approximately 80% of respondents considered gender roles as significant in relationships.

Significant Aspects in Marriage

Trust and personality were identified as the two most significant aspects of marriage.Some younger respondents specifically highlighted money as significant in marriage.

Comfort with Open Communication in Relationships

Older respondents generally expressed discomfort with open communication, while younger respondents were comfortable with it.

Importance of Cultural or Religious Compatibility in Marriage

A vast majority (96%) considered cultural or religious compatibility as significant, but the remaining 4% (most of whom were younger) believed otherwise

Importance of Having Children in Marriage

80% of respondents considered having children as important in the context of marriage.ounger respondents showed a greater variation in opinions, including neutrality or considering it as not important.

Perception of Societal Empathy Over Time

52% of respondents believed that societal empathy has declined over the years.Others had varying opinions, with some stating that empathy has remained the same or increased.
